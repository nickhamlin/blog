About Me

I’m an experienced data wonk here to help social good organizations across the globe maximize their impact. Nonprofits and social entrepreneurs do great work every day, but many don’t have the resources, tools, or experience to make sense of their data. That’s where I come in. Finding creative ways to make development data accessible, understandable, and actionable for everyone is what gets me out of bed in the morning

I’ve got a knack for learning new tools to tackle new problems. Naturally, this comes in handy when making strategic decisions about how best to support 3000+ NGOs in 160+ countries, combatting fraud on a website that processes millions worth of transactions per year, or hacking together new ways of extracting meaning out of the aid sector’s famously complex and messy data.

When I’m not writing code, crunching numbers, or telling stories with data, I'm a proud folk music street performer, amateur haiku poet, and worm farmer.
